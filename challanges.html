What are the Challanges of Open Science ?

Intellectual Property

Open science can give the real tension related to patent right and intellectual property in the current system it can create privacy issues. The actual concern rises with the e right to commercial exploitation and its protection through intellectual property rights (IPR), mainly patents.

In fields where intellectual property rights and patents are crucial, open science may be perceived as a threat to innovation and commercialization. Researchers and organizations may be less inclined to invest in research if they cannot protect their discoveries.

Ethical Culture

Open science can raise ethical dilemmas, such as how to handle data that could potentially be used for harmful purposes. It can also raise issues to violation of raising of fundamental ethical principle of privacy of the unaware community of people.
